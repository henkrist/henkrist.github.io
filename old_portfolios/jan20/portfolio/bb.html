BlackBoard 2.0

BlackBoard is the NTNU's chosen learning platform, but it is due for a redesign.
In the subject Design 3 - Use, my class was split into groups and tasked with redesigning the platform from a user centred point of view.

The course Design 3 is all about teaching us the theories about user centred design and how to use various techniques to solve the right problem to the right user.
By redesigning your school‚Äôs learning platform, we were given the opportunity to try out these theories in practice.

The BlackBoard that exist today packs a lot of features in a strange hierarchy and an unfinished interface. Students and teachers at NTNU finds the platform to be overwhelming and difficult to use. Most of them have learned their own ways of navigating around.
We had the opportunity to listen to them and create something better.

After several rounds of interviews and focus groups, my team and I were able to make some rough sketches based on the wishes and demands from the users. These were sketches we could use for further testing.

Testing the papers prototypes reviled that most users wished for better navigation between current subjects. Based on this, we could make hypotheses to test this assumption.

In the end, the interface is delivered as a poster and a notebook documenting the prosess.
